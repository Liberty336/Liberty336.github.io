So you're thinking of joining Liberty336?



Before you begin the process, you should ask yourself certain questions.



1. Why do you want to join?
2. What are you expecting to do while in?
3. What are you expecting to get in return?
4. What skills/expertise do you currently have?
5. What skills/expertise would you like to gain?
6. Etc.



At the end of the day, Liberty336 does not want you to move forward with the membership process unless you are serious. Please do not ask to join just so you can have some "buddies to train with". That is not what Liberty336 is for. Liberty336 is for serving the people of the 336 of North Carolina, and engaging in various activites.

Some activities come with risk.

There are many veterans who tell stories of times their recruiter lied to them. You won't have to worry about Liberty336 lying to recruit you, we will tell you the good, the bad, and the ugly up front.

The Bad

Here are the things main things that many find most unpleasant, we'll jump right into it.

1. You won't get paid for simply being a member.
2. We're not a Government entity, so you won't be seen as "official".
3. You will have to adapt to red tape.
4. You will have to buy most, if not all, of your gear.
