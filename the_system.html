The System

One of the most misunderstood topics that we share with the public is the topic of "The System".

"The System", is made up of smaller "systems". For example, you have:

1. The Financial system 2. The Educational system 3. The Manufacturing system 4. The Food production system 5. The Legal system 6. Nutrition rate (rate of replenishment) 7. Military system 8. etc (the list goes on)

Let "System" with a capital S refer to the "full stack" System, and let "system" with a lowercase s refer to an individual system, such as the Financial system.

Every civilization has a full stack System, it needs one in order to function. When each system is functioning properly, the civilization has a state of homeostasis just as a human body does. However, if any of these systems gets too far out of normal calibration, then problems arise, and this causes a "ripple effect" among the entire civilization. For example, if the food production system gets negatively affected, then people go hungry or starve. The Holodomor in the Soviet Union was a man made problem that affected the USSR's food production system, and many starved to death.

The importance of maintaining "homeostasis" in a System was well understood by leaders who founded a nation
